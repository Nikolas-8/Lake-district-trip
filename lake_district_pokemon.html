The journey begins! Roughly 5½ hours north through the heart of England, with a lunch stop along the way.

Arrival in Portinscale

Tiny lakeside village just west of Keswick. Drop bags, unpack, kettle on. Basecamp for the next four nights.

Pokédex Fact: Portinscale's name comes from Old Norse — Portarscali, meaning "the hut of the harlot." Locals understandably prefer the Viking translation.
⛏️ Afternoon · Little Langdale
3:15–4:45 PMWalk

Slater's Bridge & Cathedral Quarry

A gentle loop through one of the Lakes' most photogenic corners. A tiny slate packhorse bridge, then into a cavernous abandoned slate mine. Wild Diglett territory! 🪨

🥾 Easy loop 📏 ~2 miles ⛰️ ~90m gain 🪨 Rock-type
Pokédex Fact: Cathedral Quarry was carved out by hand in the 1800s — the main chamber is so vast it has natural acoustics good enough that string quartets occasionally perform inside. Pack head torches for the side tunnels (and look out for sleeping Zubat 🦇).
🌿 Late afternoon · Rydal
5:30–6:45 PMGarden

Rydal Hall Gardens & The Grot

Free-to-wander Italianate gardens with a 17th-century stone hut framing a waterfall through its window — possibly the earliest "viewing station" in England. Perfect Oddish habitat. 🌱

🌿 Grass-type zone 🌸 Free entry
Pokédex Fact: "The Grot" is thought to be Britain's oldest purpose-built viewing structure (c. 1668). Wordsworth, Coleridge, and Constable all sketched the same view through its little window.
6:45–7:30 PMSwim

Rydal Water · Wild Swim

Small, shallow, gloriously calm lake. Enter from the southern shore by the cave. Mid-June water temp typically 15–17°C. Watch for wild Poliwag 💧

🏊 Wild swim 🌡️ ~16°C 💧 Water-type
Pokédex Fact: Wordsworth lived at Rydal Mount just up the hill for 37 years until his death. He swam here regularly — apparently the original cold-water wellness influencer.
8:30 PMHome

Back to Portinscale

Dinner, wine, sleep. Tomorrow's the big one — the Pokémon League awaits! 💤

8:30 AM
– 2:30 PMThe hike

Helvellyn via Striding Edge

England's third-highest peak (950m) and arguably its finest summit. The classic route ascends via the knife-edge arête of Striding Edge — proper Onix country. Exposed scrambling, jaw-dropping drops, and a summit plateau the size of a football pitch.

🥾 Strenuous 📏 ~8.5 miles ⛰️ 950m gain 🔴 Grade 1 scramble 🪨 Rock-type Boss
Pokédex Fact: In 1926, John Leeming and Bert Hinkler landed a small aeroplane on Helvellyn's summit — the first plane landing on a British mountain. A memorial stone near the top marks the spot. Also: don't underestimate the descent. The drop from Striding Edge has caused more accidents than the ascent.
💦 Afternoon · Cool-down at the falls
3:00–4:00 PMWaterfall

Aira Force

A 65-foot waterfall in a mossy ravine ten minutes north of Glenridding. Easy paths, stone footbridges above & below the falls. Legs will thank you for the gentle stroll. Psyduck-approved confusion levels: minimal. 🦆

🥾 Easy 📏 1 mile loop ⛰️ 50m gain 💧 Water-type
Pokédex Fact: "Aira" comes from the Old Norse eyrr-á — "gravel-bank river." Wordsworth's Daffodils ("I wandered lonely as a cloud…") was inspired by daffodils he saw on the shores of nearby Ullswater, right below these falls.
5:30 PMHome

Return to Portinscale · Free time & dinner

Hot showers. Aching legs. Possibly the best meal of your life because you've earned it. 🍝

⚡ Option B · if weather's iffy

Fairfield Horseshoe + Grasmere

A classic ridge horseshoe out of Ambleside — 11 miles, 900m ascent, eight summits in one continuous loop. Finish with gingerbread in Grasmere (the village is famous for it since 1854). For the truly evolved trainers. 🏔️

🥾 Strenuous 📏 11 miles ⛰️ 900m gain

– 3:00 PMLake day

Derwentwater via Friar's Crag · Swim & Paddle

A gentle lakeside ramble from Keswick along Derwentwater's eastern shore. Friar's Crag was John Ruskin's favourite view in the world. Kayaks & paddleboards available for hire — channel your inner Lapras. 🛶

🥾 Very easy 📏 ~3 miles ⛰️ flat 🏊 Swim spots 🛶 £27–£55 hire
Pokédex Fact: Derwentwater has four small wooded islands. One, Derwent Isle, has a private house on it — the only inhabited island in the lake, owned by the National Trust and rented to one family. They commute by boat. (Definitely a Lapras trainer.)
3:00 PM
onwardsFree time

Keswick & Chill

Wander Keswick town. Bookshops, the Pencil Museum (yes, really — the graphite pencil was invented here), pubs, the lakeshore. Nap-friendly. ⚡

Pokédex Fact: In 1564, a colossal deposit of pure graphite was discovered in nearby Borrowdale — the only one of its kind ever found. Keswick became the world's pencil capital, and the world's longest pencil (7.91 metres) lives in the Pencil Museum.

Buttermere Circuit · Swim & Picnic

A near-flat 4½-mile loop around possibly the prettiest lake in England. Dramatic fells (Haystacks, Fleetwith Pike) rise straight from the water. Bring sandwiches, swim costume, and a paperback. Elegant Vaporeon energy. 💙

🥾 Easy 📏 4.5 miles ⛰️ ~50m gain 🏊 Wild swim 💧 Water-type
Pokédex Fact: Alfred Wainwright — the man who wrote the definitive seven-volume guide to every Lakeland fell — asked for his ashes to be scattered on Haystacks, the fell rising above Buttermere's southern shore. "If you should get a bit of grit in your boot as you are crossing Haystacks," he wrote, "please treat it with respect. It might be me."
3:00–6:30 PMChill

Return to Portinscale · Recharge

Back to basecamp. Showers, snacks, possibly a nap. You'll need energy for sunset. 💤

🌅 Evening · the showpiece
7:45 PM
– 10:30 PMSunset hike

Catbells at Sunset

The most beloved little fell in the Lakes. A short, sharp climb up a grassy ridge directly above Derwentwater — and at golden hour, with the sun setting over the western fells, it's properly magical. Sunset on 16 June is around 9:45 PM. Pure Togepi wonder. ✨

🥾 Moderate 📏 ~3.5 miles ⛰️ 410m gain 🌅 Sunset 21:45
Pokédex Fact: "Catbells" doesn't refer to cats at all — it's thought to come from Cat Bield, meaning "shelter of the wildcat." Wildcats roamed the Lakes until the 18th century. Pack head torches for the descent — it'll be dark by the time you're back at the car. (Beware of wild Zubat after dark 🦇)
